Design community

There are 300 designers in government.

This community exists to support people to design public services which are usable, accessible and consistent for users.

10 heads of design and GDS's design team support lead the community.

Design community page

Approach to design

Design in government is user-centred. Meaning all design decisions should be informed by user needs which come from user research.

Start with needs is one of our 10 design principles.

Prototyping

GOV.UK prototype kit provides a simple way to make interactive prototypes that look like pages on GOV.UK.

These prototypes can be used to show ideas to people you work with, and to do user research.

As a result years of testing and sharing, there are patterns to reuse in your service. For addresses in forms or asking about gender and sex.

Reusing these patterns will save you time and effort working on problems which someone has already solved.

Training

GDS provide training for designers in government. Training last three days and is held everyone six weeks at GDS in London.

The training covers:

  • how to use the prototyping kit
  • user research
  • case studies
  • graphic design
  • service design
  • user journey mapping
  • history of design and digital in government

Email Olivia Streatham to book a place.

Get advice

If you want to advice or to discuss your work you can:

  • email the design mailing list
  • use the cross government design Slack channel
  • write in the design pattern wiki
  • arrange a design crit with one of the GDS design team

Cross government design meeting

Every 8 weeks designers in government meet up.

The meeting includes:

  • updates from head of design and GDS
  • show and tells from several service teams
  • guest speaker from outside government
  • time to hang out with other designers

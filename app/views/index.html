What you're part of

There are 300 designers in government. All the designers are part of a design community.

The design community supports everyone who's designing public services to make them usable, accessible and consistent for users.

10 heads of design in departments across government and GDS's design team lead the community.

Design community page

How we do things

Design in government is user-centred. This means that every design decision should be based on user needs, and those user needs should all come from user research.

Start with needs is one of our 10 design principles.

Find prototype kit and patterns

GOV.UK prototype kit provides a simple way to make interactive prototypes that look like pages on GOV.UK.

Use prototypes to show ideas to anyone you work with, and test those ideas with users.

Use our tested patterns when you're building your service, for example, addresses in forms or how to ask users about gender and sex.

Reusing these patterns will save you time and effort, for example when you're working on problems which someone has already solved.

Get training

GDS provide training for designers in government. You can get 3 days of training - sessions are held every 6 weeks at GDS in London.

Training covers:

  • how to use the prototyping kit
  • user research
  • case studies
  • graphic design
  • service design
  • user journey mapping
  • history of design and digital in government

Email Olivia Streatham to book a place.

Get advice

If you want to advice or to discuss your work you can:

  • email the design mailing list
  • use the cross government design Slack channel
  • write in the design pattern wiki
  • arrange a design crit with one of the GDS design team

Go to design meetup

Every 8 weeks designers in government meet up.

The meeting includes:

  • updates from head of design and GDS
  • show and tells from several service teams
  • guest speaker from outside government
  • time to hang out with other designers
